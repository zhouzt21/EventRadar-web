Motivation

In urban civilian airspace, small rotor UAVs have become a major illegal-flight concern: they are flexible, low-cost, and can appear near airports or public infrastructure with little warning. Radar and radio-frequency monitoring are often hard to miniaturize and may introduce electromagnetic interference, motivating a sensing system that is passive, discreet, and easy to deploy.

A practical counter-UAV system must provide early warning, support threat assessment, and maintain wide-area coverage. At kilometer-scale range, these goals collide: the drone may shrink to only a few pixels, useful signals are buried in noise, and a long-focus lens creates a narrow field of view where targets are easily lost.

EventRadar addresses this tension through propeller-aware sensing. It treats propeller rotational speed as a bridge from hidden flight command and payload to observable high-frequency event footprints, enabling long-range detection, RPM-based inference, and active gimbal tracking in one pipeline.

System Introduction

Propeller Speed as the Bridge Between Intrinsic and External

EventRadar treats the propeller as a physical signature: internal command and payload affect rotational speed, while rotational speed determines observable event footprints and flight state.

A

Long-Range Detection

When spatial shape fades to only a few pixels, EventRadar trades time for space. It uses microsecond event resolution to capture periodic propeller fluctuations and reconstruct sparse frequency signatures from noisy event streams.

B

Intrinsic Inference

With estimated rotational speed ω, EventRadar links observation to physics. The thrust relation Ti ≈ kTωi2 enables payload and intent inference for interpretable threat assessment at long range.

C

Wide-Area Tracking

A portable two-axis gimbal expands coverage despite the narrow long-lens field of view. Event trailing guidance estimates escape direction and speed from residual event traces, helping the gimbal recover fast-moving targets.

Experiment Demonstration

From Kilometric Detection to Active Tracking

The demo validates EventRadar across detection, physical inference, and active tracking.

A. Km-Range Drone Detection

Robust detection beyond conventional visual range

At 300 m, EventRadar achieves about 60x the mAP of an RGB baseline and 18x that of an event-based baseline. It maintains stable detection and tracking up to 1500 m.

B. Drone Weight Estimation

Inferring payload through propeller RPM

Propeller RPM provides a measurable bridge to payload inference, keeping weight-estimation relative error within 2% for interpretable threat assessment.

C. Active Drone Tracking

Recovering targets from event trails

Low-latency event trails estimate escape direction and speed, guiding the gimbal to reacquire targets in a narrow field of view.
